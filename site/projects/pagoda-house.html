Overview

Lead Architecture & Interior Design Consultants

The iconic Pagoda House on Al Wasl Road, inspired by traditional Chinese architecture, has been carefully restored to preserve its unique heritage while adapting it for contemporary living.

With no original drawings available, the team meticulously documented and rediscovered the structure before reimagining its interiors as bright, open, minimalist spaces that complement the ornate exterior. The result is a timeless home that seamlessly blends cultural heritage with modern design.

The residence, inherited by the client from his father, holds significant sentimental and historical value — demanding a sensitive approach that respected its legacy while adapting it to the way the family lives today.

Master bed extension

New roof, matched to a legacy

The master bedroom is a new build, and its roof had to match the roof of the existing structure exactly. With no archive to lean on, the drawings were produced through detailed site inspection and by making use of the existing SketchUp models.

  • Coordinated MEP and HVAC systems using detailed site investigations where original drawings were unavailable
  • Studied site constraints and mapped the structural layouts
  • Developed the 3D design in SketchUp to understand beam and column sizes
  • Coordinated with the 3D team for final renders
